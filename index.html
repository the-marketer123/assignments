New Years Travelogue in Russia

My trip to the New Years in Russia, Jan 7, 2025

As the plane descended into Moscow's Sheremetyevo Airport, I could hardly contain my excitement. I'd heard so much about the grandeur of Russia's New Year's celebrations, and now I was about to experience it firsthand. The city greeted me with its icy embrace. Snowflakes danced in the air, painting the city in a blanket of white. The temperature was a sharp contrast to the mild winters I was used to, but it only added to the enchantment. Moscow's streets were aglow with festive lights, and the scent of pine trees filled the air. On New Year's Eve, I found myself in Red Square, the heart of Moscow. The historic square was transformed into a winter wonderland, with an enormous Christmas tree standing proudly in the center. Stalls lined the square, offering warm beverages, traditional Russian snacks, and handmade crafts. I couldn't resist the allure of a steaming cup of mulled wine as I strolled through the bustling crowd. As midnight approached, the atmosphere grew electric. People from all walks of life gathered in anticipation, their breath visible in the crisp night air. The clock tower of the Kremlin began to chime, each toll sending a ripple of excitement through the crowd. When the final stroke of midnight echoed through the square, fireworks erupted in a dazzling display of color and light, illuminating the night sky. The fireworks were breathtaking, but it was the sense of unity and joy among the people that truly moved me. Strangers embraced, sharing well-wishes for the year ahead. I found myself caught up in the moment, joining in the chorus of "S Novym Godom!" – Happy New Year! The celebrations continued long into the night. I joined a group of locals who invited me to a nearby bar. Inside, it was warm and lively, with music playing and people dancing. I sampled traditional Russian dishes like Olivier salad and blini, washed down with shots of vodka. Laughter and cheer filled the room, and I felt a deep sense of camaraderie with my newfound friends. As the first light of dawn broke over the city, I made my way back to my hotel, my heart full and my spirits high. Moscow had given me an unforgettable New Year's experience, one that I would cherish forever.

Gunnar

8th grade student at Oasis Middle School
